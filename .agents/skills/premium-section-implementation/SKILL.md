# premium-section-implementation

## Purpose
Turn premium brand intent into concrete, buildable public website sections that feel elegant, strategic, and conversion-aware without becoming generic or overdesigned.

## Use when
Use for designing, refining, or implementing homepage sections and other public-facing website sections in `apps/website`.

## Do not use when
Do not use for backend logic, internal tools, CRM workflows, or tasks unrelated to public section design.

## Project truths
- Warszawski Czas is a luxury boutique, not a marketplace.
- Sections must support both prestige and lead generation.
- The site must feel premium, curated, and confident.
- Sections should not all look or behave the same.
- The emotional role of a section depends on the emotional role of the page it belongs to.
- Dark green and gold are subtle accents, not dominant visual noise.

## Required dependency on page intent
Before designing any section, determine the page intent using:
- `wc-design-direction-and-page-intent`

A premium section on the homepage must not be designed the same way as:
- a section on products
- a section on hidden collection
- a section on services
- a section on boutique
- a section on contact

## Required dependency on visual grammar
When deciding composition, spacing, rhythm, media, or layout tension, use:
- `wc-visual-grammar-and-composition`

Do not solve composition problems with decoration.
Do not solve weak sections with extra boxes, effects, or visual clutter.

## Section design rules
- Every section must have a clear role.
- Every section must move the page forward.
- Each section must either:
  - expand desire
  - increase trust
  - clarify the offer
  - guide the next step
- Avoid filler sections with no real persuasive or experiential purpose.
- Build sections around one dominant message, not many medium-strength messages.
- Keep supporting text controlled and readable.
- CTA should feel earned and natural.

## Premium implementation principles
- Lead with clear hierarchy.
- Prefer strong composition over decorative UI.
- Let spacing create status.
- Use restrained contrast.
- Use media purposefully, not constantly.
- Allow some sections to be quieter if the page needs rhythm.
- Use premium typography and calm layout, not fake-luxury effects.

## Layout and composition rules
- A section must fit into the visual rhythm of the page.
- Do not stack multiple sections with identical structure.
- Alternate text-led and media-led moments where useful.
- Keep alignment logic clean.
- Avoid over-fragmented layouts.
- Prefer broad, confident blocks over many small containers.
- Use asymmetry only when it improves sophistication and scanability.

## Copy-structure rules
- Headline first, then controlled supporting copy.
- Do not let copy become too long unless the page genuinely needs depth.
- Avoid empty slogans.
- Use language that feels premium, calm, and specific.
- Section copy should match the role of the page:
  - more aspirational on homepage
  - more selective on products
  - more suggestive on hidden collection
  - more trust-based on services
  - more experiential on boutique
  - more concierge-like on contact

## CTA rules
- CTA energy should match page intent.
- Homepage can carry stronger CTA energy.
- Hidden collection should invite rather than push.
- Services should reassure rather than pressure.
- Contact should feel open, elegant, and natural.
- Avoid repeating identical CTA treatment everywhere.

## Media rules
- Every media element must serve atmosphere, trust, product desire, or place experience.
- Avoid random stock-like visuals.
- If real media is missing, placeholders must still feel premium.
- Use visual restraint: one strong image is better than three weak ones.
- Do not overload sections with decorative imagery.

## Anti-patterns
- Premium sections that are only “pretty” but strategically empty
- Repetitive card layouts across many sections
- Overuse of gold accents and dark backgrounds
- Generic luxury clichés
- Startup-style rounded UI with premium copy pasted on top
- Dense sections with weak hierarchy
- The same section structure reused page after page
- Over-animated sections
- Visually loud sections that reduce class

## Required questions before implementing a section
- What is the role of this section on this page?
- What should the user feel here?
- Does this section need intensity or restraint?
- Is the structure right for this page, not just visually attractive?
- Is this section helping rhythm or flattening the page?
- Would this section still feel premium if decorative effects were removed?

## Guardrails
- Do not design sections in isolation from page intent.
- Do not make all premium sections equally expressive.
- Do not use decoration to fake depth.
- Do not add complexity before improving hierarchy, spacing, and message clarity.
- Do not let conversion pressure weaken prestige.

## Checklist
- Is the page intent clear first?
- Is the section role clear?
- Does the composition fit the page rhythm?
- Is the section visually strong without being loud?
- Is the CTA placement natural?
- Does the section feel premium, not generic?

- Page context
